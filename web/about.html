What is GBPF?

GBPF is a GBP-pegged stablecoin on the Base network, fully backed by US-dollar reserves and priced honestly against them.

The short version

GBPF tracks the British pound. One GBPF is designed to be worth roughly one pound's worth of value. You buy it by sending USDS (a US-dollar stablecoin) and you can sell it back for USDS at any time.

Behind every GBPF in circulation sits real US-dollar collateral, held in an on-chain vault and earning yield. GBPF is priced against that collateral using a live GBP/USD exchange-rate oracle — so its dollar price moves with the pound, exactly as it should.

No hard peg — hard solvency. GBPF doesn't promise a fixed exchange rate it can't keep. Instead it promises that the collateral backing it is always accounted for honestly, and the price you get always reflects what's actually in the vault.

How it's backed

Every GBPF is backed by USDS / sUSDS — Sky's (formerly MakerDAO) US-dollar stablecoin and its yield-bearing version — held in an on-chain Vault contract on Base.

  • When you buy GBPF, your USDS goes into the Vault and new GBPF is minted to you.
  • The Vault holds the reserves as sUSDS, so the collateral earns the Sky Savings Rate while it sits there.
  • When you sell GBPF, it is burned and the matching USDS is paid out of the Vault.

The backing is fully on-chain and verifiable. Anyone can read the Vault's reserves and GBPF's total supply directly from the contracts (linked below) — you don't have to take anyone's word for it.

How it's stabilised against USDS

GBPF is a pound; USDS is a dollar. So GBPF is not meant to trade one-for-one with USDS — its USDS price floats with the real GBP/USD rate. If a pound is worth $1.27, then one GBPF is worth about 1.27 USDS.

That rate comes from a Chainlink GBP/USD oracle, read as a time-weighted average (TWAP) to smooth out short-term spikes and make price manipulation impractical. Every buy and sell is settled at the oracle rate against the Vault — not against a thin liquidity pool that can be pushed around.

On top of the oracle rate there's a solvency spread that keeps the system honest when the dollar reserves don't quite cover the pound liabilities (which can happen purely because GBP/USD moved):

  • At or above 100% backing: no spread — you trade at the oracle rate, plus the standard fees.
  • Below 100% backing: GBPF is priced at a small discount. Buying gets cheaper (you're rewarded for adding collateral) and selling takes a haircut (redeemers share the shortfall fairly instead of first-out-gets-everything).

The discount grows smoothly as backing falls and is capped at 5%. This is what lets GBPF stay solvent and fairly priced through FX swings, without ever pretending the reserves are bigger than they are.

Why a non-fiat-backed pound?

Most "stablecoins" are an IOU from a company that holds cash in a bank and promises you can redeem it. GBPF is the opposite: a pound built entirely on-chain, backed by on-chain reserves. That changes what it can offer.

No censorship
There's no issuer sitting on a pile of fiat who can freeze, blacklist or claw back your balance. You hold GBPF in your own wallet and move it freely.
Honest solvency
The price always reflects what's actually in the vault. No marketing peg propped up until it breaks — if backing dips, the price says so, openly.
Real-time proof of reserves
Reserves and supply are readable on-chain any second of any day — not a quarterly attestation from an accounting firm you have to trust.
No banking counterparty
Reserves are yield-bearing sUSDS on-chain, not cash in a commercial bank that can fail or freeze — the failure mode that broke other stablecoins' pegs.
A pound, free of the rails
GBP-denominated value you can hold and send 24/7, worldwide, without a UK bank account or anyone's permission — no central bank gatekeeping the plumbing.
Deep, direct liquidity
Swaps mint() and redeem() straight against the vault at the oracle rate — there's no order book or AMM pool to run dry or slip. Buying never runs out; selling is limited only by the reserves themselves, and even then you're priced fairly, not slipped.
Self-custody & composable
Your keys, your GBPF. It's a standard on-chain token, so it plugs straight into wallets and DeFi — no gated app or withdrawal windows.

The trade-off is honesty about risk: a non-fiat-backed pound doesn't promise a rate it can't keep (see the risks below). What it promises is that the backing is real, visible, and yours to verify.

What it costs

Two fees apply on top of the oracle rate:

  • A 20bp (0.20%) swap fee charged inside the swap itself.
  • A small flat service fee (0.05 USDS + a 0.10 USDS batch fee that's split across everyone swapping at the same time) to cover keeper gas.

Changed your mind before your swap is processed? Your funds sit untouched at your deposit address until the batch runs, and you can pull them back in full with refund() — no fee deducted.

Honest risks

GBPF is designed to be transparent about how it can lose value. The main risks:

FX / solvency
Reserves are in USD but liabilities are in GBP. A rising pound lowers backing below 100%, which triggers the redemption haircut.
Collateral
Backing depends on USDS / sUSDS holding their own dollar peg and on Sky's solvency.
Oracle
Pricing relies on the Chainlink GBP/USD feed. If it reports unhealthy, swaps pause until it recovers.
Smart contract
As with any on-chain protocol, the code itself carries risk. Verify the contracts before you send funds.
